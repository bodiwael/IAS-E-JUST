Free Courses for IAS Members

The IEEE Industry Applications Society (IAS) offers a variety of free courses designed to enhance the knowledge and skills of professionals in the field of industrial applications. These courses cover essential topics such as electrical safety, energy management, power electronics, and industrial power systems. They are particularly beneficial for engineers, researchers, and technicians who work with industrial electrical systems, renewable energy, and manufacturing technologies. Below is a list of some valuable free courses available to IAS members.

1. Reducing Risk When Performing Energized Work on Batteries

Description: This course covers best practices for safely performing energized work on battery systems. It discusses hazards, risk mitigation techniques, and safe working procedures when dealing with high-energy battery systems.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: With the growing use of battery storage in industrial power systems, understanding safety protocols is crucial. This course is particularly useful for engineers and technicians working in energy storage, data centers, and renewable energy sectors.

Access the course here

2. Energy-Aware Operation of Data Centers Towards Low-Carbon Future

Description: This course focuses on energy-efficient data center operations and strategies to reduce carbon emissions. Topics include power management, cooling technologies, and sustainable energy integration.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: The demand for sustainable solutions in industrial settings, particularly in data centers, is increasing. This course is valuable for professionals involved in facility management, IT infrastructure, and energy optimization.

Access the course here

3. Modeling DC Arc Physics and Applications for DC Arc Flash Risk Assessment

Description: This course provides an in-depth look at DC arc physics and how it applies to assessing arc flash risks in industrial environments. It covers modeling techniques, safety standards, and preventive measures.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: Arc flash incidents pose serious risks in industrial electrical systems, especially in renewable energy, manufacturing, and transportation sectors where DC power is widely used. Understanding these risks helps improve workplace safety.

Access the course here

4. Grounding and Bonding Practices: Principles and Applications for Electrical Safety and Lightning Protection

Description: This course explores the principles of grounding and bonding, key to ensuring electrical safety and lightning protection. It covers grounding techniques, design considerations, and compliance with safety codes.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: Proper grounding and bonding are critical in preventing electrical hazards in industrial facilities, substations, and power distribution systems. This course is essential for engineers and safety professionals working in industrial electrical design.

Access the course here

5. Analysis and Design of Series LC Resonant Pulse Assisted Soft Switching Current Fed DC/DC Converters

Description: This technical course examines the design and application of soft-switching DC/DC converters, with a focus on series LC resonant pulse-assisted techniques for improving efficiency and performance.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: DC/DC converters play a crucial role in industrial power systems, renewable energy applications, and electric vehicle technology. Engineers designing power electronics and energy-efficient industrial systems will benefit from this course.

Access the course here

6. Occupational Electrical Safety: Advanced Topics in Engineering and Safety Management

Description: This workshop covers advanced topics in electrical safety, including hazard assessment, risk reduction techniques, and compliance with safety regulations in industrial workplaces.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: Electrical safety is a critical concern in industrial settings where high-voltage equipment and machinery are in operation. This course is beneficial for engineers, safety managers, and industrial facility operators.

Access the course here

7. Grid Interactive Power Converter Controls and Their Fault Ride-Through Capability

Description: This course focuses on control strategies for grid-interactive power converters and their ability to ride through faults while maintaining system stability.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: Grid stability is essential for industrial power systems, especially in smart grids, renewable energy integration, and large-scale industrial power management. This course is ideal for engineers working in power electronics and energy systems.

Access the course here

8. ScholarOne Manuscripts TCPRC/AE Training

Description: This webinar provides training on the ScholarOne Manuscripts system, a tool used for managing the peer review process in technical journals.

Relevance to Industrial Applications: Engineers and researchers involved in publishing technical papers on industrial applications can benefit from this training. It is particularly useful for those contributing to IEEE and other scientific publications.

These courses provide valuable insights into industrial power systems, electrical safety, and energy efficiency. By participating in these training sessions, professionals can enhance their expertise, improve workplace safety, and stay updated with advancements in industrial applications. This is one of the many benefits of being an IAS member, as these high-quality courses are available at no cost to support your professional development.
